The hidden cost isn't the hours — it's that you stop

The real problem with the manual stack isn't any single step. It's that the whole thing is annoying enough that you run it once, file the number away, and never re-measure. And a PMF score you measure once is worse than useless — it gives you a stale snapshot and false confidence while fit quietly shifts underneath you.

Manual PMF tracking doesn't fail because the math is hard. It fails because you give up after round two — and the trend is the entire point.

When Typeform + spreadsheets is the better choice

The DIY stack isn't wrong for everyone. Stick with Typeform and a spreadsheet when:

  • You already live in Typeform and need its polished, branded forms for lots of use cases beyond PMF.
  • Product-market fit is a genuine one-off measurement, not something you'll revisit each month.
  • You're comfortable in spreadsheets and actually want manual control over how the score is cut.
  • Form design flexibility matters to you more than an automated, tracked metric.

The trade-off is time and consistency: the manual stack works until the month you're slammed and skip it — and the trend line you needed for your raise never got built.
